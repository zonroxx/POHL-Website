The Founders

Ms. Pamela Brown-Blackburn, Esq.

President and Secretary

With over 25 years of experience in combatting domestic violence, sexual assault, sex trafficking, labor abuse, and human trafficking. She brings extensive expertise in aiding victims and driving systemic change throughout the Pacific Region.

Mr. Anthony M. Babauta

Vice President

Former Assistant Secretary of the Office of Insular Affairs with vast knowledge of criminal issues throughout the Pacific Region and the U.S. government's efforts to end abuses and assist local governments.

Ms. Laurie Peterka

Treasurer

Dedicated her career to issues and concerns facing the Pacific Region, with extensive travel and experience throughout the region. Her expertise strengthens POHL's regional networks and collaborative efforts.

Board Leadership

Each member of the Board of Directors brings unique and extensive experience in addressing the criminal issues facing the Pacific Region. Their combined expertise in legal services, government affairs, and regional collaboration enables POHL to effectively assist victims and drive meaningful change throughout the Pacific community.

Together, they maintain strong networks and contacts that serve POHL's ability to not only assist victims of these crimes but also to make a lasting impact through collaborative efforts within the local community and across the Pacific Region.
